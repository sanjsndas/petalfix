About Petalfix

Our Story

Founded in 2010 by master horticulturist Emily Thompson, Petalfix began as a small neighborhood plant clinic dedicated to solving common plant health issues. What started as a modest operation with just three employees has blossomed into one of the most trusted names in floral care across the tri-state area, with a team of 45 certified specialists.

Our approach combines cutting-edge plant pathology research with time-tested gardening wisdom. Each of our specialists undergoes rigorous training in plant physiology, soil science, and integrated pest management. We've successfully treated over 12,000 cases of plant disease and nutritional disorders, helping everything from delicate orchids to robust rose bushes thrive.

At Petalfix, we believe that healthy plants create healthier living spaces and happier people. Our mission extends beyond treatment - we're committed to educating our clients about sustainable gardening practices that promote long-term plant health while minimizing environmental impact.

Our Specialized Services

Comprehensive solutions tailored to your plants' specific needs

Floral Disease Control

Our comprehensive disease management program begins with precise diagnosis using microscopic analysis and DNA testing when needed. We treat all common floral diseases including powdery mildew, botrytis blight, rust fungi, and bacterial leaf spots. Our treatment protocols combine organic fungicides, biological controls, and cultural practices to restore plant health without harming beneficial organisms.

  • Advanced pathogen identification
  • Custom treatment plans
  • Preventative care programs
  • Follow-up monitoring

Nutrient Management

Proper nutrition is the foundation of plant health. Our soil scientists conduct comprehensive soil tests measuring pH, macro and micronutrients, organic matter content, and cation exchange capacity. We then create customized fertilizer blends and amendment schedules tailored to your specific plants and soil conditions. Our programs address common deficiencies while preventing nutrient toxicities and runoff pollution.

  • Complete soil analysis
  • Seasonal fertilization plans
  • Organic amendment options
  • Foliar feeding programs

Eco-Pest Solutions

Our integrated pest management (IPM) approach focuses on long-term prevention through biological control, habitat manipulation, and resistant plant varieties. We use pesticides only as a last resort, preferring organic and low-toxicity options when needed. Our specialists can identify and treat over 200 common garden pests while preserving beneficial insects like pollinators and predators that keep pest populations in check naturally.

  • Pest identification
  • Biological controls
  • Organic treatments
  • Monitoring programs

Seasonal Gardening Tips

Expert advice to keep your garden thriving year-round

Spring Planting

Start your garden with cool-season crops like lettuce and peas. Ensure soil is well-drained and enriched with compost. Consider companion planting to maximize space and deter pests.

Summer Care

Water deeply and regularly during hot months. Mulching helps retain moisture and suppress weeds. Monitor for pests and diseases, as they can thrive in warm weather.

Fall Preparation

Prepare your garden for winter by cleaning up debris and planting cover crops to improve soil health. Consider planting perennials that will bloom in the spring.

Winter Care

Protect sensitive plants with frost cloths and ensure proper drainage to prevent root rot. Use mulch to insulate the soil and prevent freeze-thaw cycles.
