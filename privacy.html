Privacy Policy

Our commitment to protecting your personal information

Last Updated: June 15, 2023

Petalfix ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website or use our services. Please read this privacy policy carefully. If you do not agree with the terms of this privacy policy, please do not access the site.

1. Information We Collect

We collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us when registering on the site, expressing interest in obtaining information about us or our services, participating in activities on the site, or otherwise contacting us. The personal information we collect may include names, email addresses, phone numbers, mailing addresses, and other similar contact data. We also collect payment information for processing transactions, though we do not store credit card details on our servers.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use personal information collected via our site for various business purposes described below. We process your personal information for these purposes in reliance on our legitimate business interests, to enter into or perform a contract with you, with your consent, and/or for compliance with our legal obligations. We use the information we collect or receive to facilitate account creation and logon process, send administrative information to you, request feedback, enable user-to-user communications, manage user accounts, send you marketing and promotional communications, deliver targeted advertising, protect our services, and respond to legal requests.

3. Sharing Your Information

We only share information with your consent, to comply with laws, to provide you with services, to protect your rights, or to fulfill business obligations. We may process or share your data based on the following legal basis: Consent when you have given us specific consent to use your personal information for a specific purpose; Legitimate Interests when it is reasonably necessary to achieve our legitimate business interests; Performance of a Contract when we have entered into a contract with you and need to process your personal information to fulfill our obligations under that contract; Legal Obligations when we are legally required to disclose your personal information to comply with applicable law, governmental requests, judicial proceedings, court orders, or legal processes.

4. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to access or store information. Specific information about how we use such technologies and how you can refuse certain cookies is set out in our Cookie Policy. Most web browsers are set to accept cookies by default. If you prefer, you can usually choose to set your browser to remove cookies and to reject cookies. If you choose to remove cookies or reject cookies, this could affect certain features or services of our site.

5. Data Retention

We will retain your personal information only for as long as is necessary for the purposes set out in this Privacy Policy. We will retain and use your information to the extent necessary to comply with our legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our policies. Retention periods will be determined taking into account the type of information that is collected and the purpose for which it is collected, bearing in mind the requirements applicable to the situation and the need to destroy outdated, unused information at the earliest reasonable time.

6. Information Security

We have implemented appropriate technical and organizational security measures designed to protect the security of any personal information we process. However, despite our safeguards and efforts to secure your information, no electronic transmission over the Internet or information storage technology can be guaranteed to be 100% secure, so we cannot promise or guarantee that hackers, cybercriminals, or other unauthorized third parties will not be able to defeat our security and improperly collect, access, steal, or modify your information.

7. International Data Transfers

We may transfer, store, and process your information in countries other than your own. Our servers are located in the United States, so if you are accessing our site from outside the United States, please be aware that your information may be transferred to, stored, and processed by us in our facilities and by those third parties with whom we may share your personal information. If you are a resident in the European Economic Area, then these countries may not necessarily have data protection laws or other similar laws as comprehensive as those in your country.

8. Children's Privacy

We do not knowingly solicit data from or market to children under 18 years of age. By using the site, you represent that you are at least 18 or that you are the parent or guardian of such a minor and consent to such minor dependent's use of the site. If we learn that personal information from users less than 18 years of age has been collected, we will deactivate the account and take reasonable measures to promptly delete such data from our records.

9. Your Privacy Rights

In some regions (like the European Economic Area), you have certain rights under applicable data protection laws. These may include the right to request access and obtain a copy of your personal information, to request rectification or erasure, to restrict the processing of your personal information, and if applicable, to data portability. In certain circumstances, you may also have the right to object to the processing of your personal information. To make such a request, please use the contact details provided below.

10. Third-Party Websites

Our site may contain links to third-party websites and applications of interest, including advertisements and external services, that are not affiliated with us. Once you have used these links to leave our site, any information you provide to these third parties is not covered by this Privacy Policy, and we cannot guarantee the safety and privacy of your information. Before visiting and providing any information to any third-party websites, you should inform yourself of the privacy policies and practices of the third party responsible for that website.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The updated version will be indicated by an updated "Revised" date and the updated version will be effective as soon as it is accessible. If we make material changes to this Privacy Policy, we may notify you either by prominently posting a notice of such changes or by directly sending you a notification. We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy frequently to be informed of how we are protecting your information.

12. Contact Us

If you have questions or comments about this Privacy Policy, please contact us at privacy@petalfix.com or by mail at the address provided below. For any requests regarding your personal data or if you wish to exercise any of your rights, you may also contact our Data Protection Officer at dpo@petalfix.com. We will respond to all legitimate requests within one month. Occasionally it may take us longer than a month if your request is particularly complex or you have made a number of requests.

13. Data Processing Agreements

We enter into data processing agreements with all third-party service providers that process personal data on our behalf. These agreements require that such parties agree to process such information based on our instructions and in compliance with both this Privacy Policy and any other appropriate confidentiality and security measures. We conduct due diligence on all third parties to ensure they meet our standards for data protection and only work with vendors who can demonstrate compliance with applicable data protection laws.

14. Automated Decision Making

We do not use fully automated decision-making that has legal or similarly significant effects on data subjects. Where we use any form of automated processing of personal data to evaluate certain personal aspects relating to a natural person, we ensure appropriate measures are in place to safeguard the data subject's rights and freedoms and legitimate interests, including at least the right to obtain human intervention, to express their point of view, and to contest the decision.

15. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach that is likely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals, we will notify affected individuals and relevant supervisory authorities without undue delay and, where feasible, within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach. Our notification will include the nature of the breach, the categories and approximate number of data subjects concerned, the likely consequences of the breach, and the measures taken or proposed to be taken to address the breach.

16. Data Protection Impact Assessments

Where a type of processing, particularly using new technologies, is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons, we will carry out a data protection impact assessment prior to the processing. The assessment will include a systematic description of the envisaged processing operations, an assessment of the necessity and proportionality of the processing, an assessment of the risks to the rights and freedoms of data subjects, and the measures envisaged to address the risks.

17. Data Minimization

We adhere to the principle of data minimization, collecting only the personal data that is adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary in relation to the purposes for which they are processed. We periodically review the data we hold and delete any information that is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected or otherwise processed. Our systems are designed to collect only the minimum amount of information required to provide our services effectively.

18. Employee Data Protection

All our employees who handle personal data are required to complete data protection training and are bound by confidentiality agreements. Access to personal data is restricted to authorized personnel only, based on job function and role requirements. We maintain detailed access logs and conduct regular audits to monitor compliance with our data protection policies. Employees are trained to recognize and report potential data breaches immediately.

19. Vendor Management

We conduct thorough due diligence on all vendors who may process personal data on our behalf. Our vendor contracts include strict data protection clauses that require compliance with applicable laws and our privacy standards. We maintain a vendor register that documents all third parties processing personal data for us, the categories of data shared, and the purposes of processing. Vendors are subject to regular audits to verify their ongoing compliance with data protection requirements.

20. Record Keeping

We maintain detailed records of our data processing activities as required by applicable data protection laws. These records include information about the categories of personal data processed, the purposes of processing, the categories of recipients to whom the data has been disclosed, and the retention periods for different categories of data. We regularly review and update these records to ensure they accurately reflect our data processing activities.

21. User Access and Control

Users have the right to access their personal data and request corrections or deletions where applicable. We provide users with the ability to update their information through their account settings or by contacting us directly. Users can also request a copy of their personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format. We will respond to such requests within the timeframe required by applicable law.

22. Marketing Communications

We may use your personal information to send you marketing communications about our services, promotions, and events. You can opt-out of receiving marketing communications at any time by following the unsubscribe instructions included in each email or by contacting us directly. We will respect your preferences and ensure that you do not receive further communications if you opt-out.

23. Data Protection Officer

We have appointed a Data Protection Officer (DPO) who is responsible for overseeing our data protection strategy and implementation. The DPO is available to address any questions or concerns regarding our data protection practices and to assist with any requests related to personal data. You can contact our DPO at dpo@petalfix.com.

24. Legal Basis for Processing

We process personal data based on various legal bases, including consent, performance of a contract, compliance with legal obligations, and legitimate interests. We ensure that we have a valid legal basis for processing personal data and that our processing activities are transparent and fair.

25. Data Protection Training

All employees receive regular training on data protection and privacy practices. This training covers topics such as data handling, security measures, and the importance of protecting personal information. We aim to foster a culture of data protection awareness within our organization.

26. Privacy by Design

We implement privacy by design principles in our business processes and systems. This means that we consider privacy and data protection from the outset of any project or initiative. We conduct privacy impact assessments to identify and mitigate potential risks to personal data.

27. Data Anonymization

We may anonymize personal data for research and analytical purposes. Anonymization involves removing identifiable information so that individuals cannot be identified from the data. This allows us to use data for insights and improvements while protecting user privacy.

28. User Consent

We obtain user consent for specific processing activities where required by law. Consent is obtained through clear and affirmative actions, and users have the right to withdraw their consent at any time. We ensure that consent requests are easy to understand and not bundled with other consents.

29. Data Portability

Users have the right to data portability, which allows them to obtain and reuse their personal data across different services. We provide users with their personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format upon request. This enables users to transfer their data to other service providers easily.

30. User Rights Under GDPR

If you are a resident of the European Economic Area (EEA), you have certain rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). These rights include the right to access, rectify, erase, restrict, object to processing, and data portability. We are committed to facilitating the exercise of these rights in accordance with applicable law.

31. Data Breach Response Plan

We have established a data breach response plan to address any potential data breaches effectively. This plan includes procedures for identifying, reporting, and managing data breaches, as well as notifying affected individuals and relevant authorities as required by law.

32. Third-Party Data Processors

We may engage third-party data processors to assist us in providing our services. These processors are required to adhere to strict data protection standards and are only permitted to process personal data in accordance with our instructions. We conduct regular audits to ensure compliance with our data protection policies.

33. User Feedback and Complaints

We value user feedback and take complaints seriously. If you have any concerns about our data protection practices or wish to lodge a complaint, please contact us using the details provided in this policy. We will investigate and respond to all complaints promptly.

34. Privacy Policy Updates

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices or applicable laws. We will notify users of any significant changes and encourage users to review this policy regularly to stay informed about our data protection practices.

36. User Consent for Data Processing

By using our services, you consent to the processing of your personal data as described in this Privacy Policy. You have the right to withdraw your consent at any time, and we will cease processing your data upon receiving such a request.

37. Data Protection Compliance

We are committed to complying with all applicable data protection laws and regulations. Our data protection practices are regularly reviewed and updated to ensure compliance with legal requirements and best practices.

38. User Rights Under CCPA

If you are a California resident, you have certain rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). These rights include the right to know what personal data we collect, the right to request deletion of your personal data, and the right to opt-out of the sale of your personal data. To exercise these rights, please contact us using the information provided in this policy.

39. Data Protection Impact Assessments

We conduct data protection impact assessments (DPIAs) for processing activities that may pose a high risk to individuals' rights and freedoms. DPIAs help us identify and mitigate potential risks associated with data processing activities.

40. Conclusion

We take your privacy seriously and are committed to protecting your personal information. This Privacy Policy outlines our practices regarding the collection, use, and protection of your data. If you have any questions or concerns, please do not hesitate to contact us.
